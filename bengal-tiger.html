The Bengal tiger

About

The Bengal tiger is a population of the Panthera tigris tigris subspecies and the nominate tiger subspecies. It ranks among the largest wild cats alive today. It is estimated to have been present in the Indian subcontinent since the Late Pleistocene for about 12,000 to 16,500 years. Wikipedia

Mass: 200 – 260 kg (Male, Adult), 100 – 160 kg (Female, Adult)

Conservation status: Endangered (Population decreasing)
Encyclopedia of Life

Scientific name: Panthera tigris tigris

Height: 90 – 110 cm (At Shoulder)
